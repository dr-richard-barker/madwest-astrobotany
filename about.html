About

One stop: rocket science and real science

MadWest Rocketry exists to close the gap between a fun launch and a useful contribution to space biology.

The vision

Thousands of students and hobbyists build high-power rockets every year. Almost none of those flights produce data anyone reuses. Meanwhile, NASA's Open Science Data Repository is hungry for well-described biology data — and the effects of launch loads, vibration, and brief altitude exposure on seeds are genuinely under-studied.

MadWest connects the two. We give builders a botanical sounding rocket and a data pathway in the same place, so a club or classroom flight ends as an open, citable dataset instead of a one-off afternoon.

What "one stop" means

  • Build — engineer a rocket that keeps a living payload alive.
  • Train — learn open-science standards in plain language.
  • Fly & measure — capture flight + biology data together.
  • Submit — hand a clean dataset to NASA OSDR.

What we stand for

🔓

Open by default

Every flight aims to produce FAIR, openly shareable data — findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable.

🛡️

Safety first

We build and fly under recognized rocketry-body rules and local law, always with certified mentors at sanctioned ranges.

🎓

Learning that lasts

Designing to a real scientific standard teaches more than any kit — students leave knowing how science is actually shared.

The honest fine print

MadWest Rocketry is an independent educational project. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by NASA. Always follow the official OSDR portal for current submission rules, and your national/local rocketry regulations for flight.
