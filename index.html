Why connect rocketry to OSDR?

NASA's Open Science Data Repository hosts spaceflight and space-relevant biology data so that anyone can reuse it. Most contributors are professional labs — but the questions a botanical sounding rocket can ask (How do seeds handle high-G launch? Acceleration plus altitude? Recovery stress?) are real, repeatable, and underexplored.

MadWest's goal is to lower the barrier: give builders a rocket and a data pathway, so a classroom or club flight ends as a properly described, openly shared dataset instead of a number scribbled in a notebook.
