Data Submission

From flight log to open dataset

You flew it, you measured it, you trained on the standards. This is the workflow that gets your dataset into NASA OSDR — described well enough that someone you'll never meet can reuse it.

Step 1 — Organize your files

A clean, predictable folder layout makes everything downstream easier. A suggested structure for one flight (one "study"):

madwest-flight-2026-XX/
├── README.md                 # what this flight was, in plain language
├── metadata/
│   ├── study.csv             # study-level: title, organism, factors
│   ├── assays.csv            # which measurements were run
│   └── samples.csv           # one row per seed/sample, flown vs. control
├── flight/
│   ├── altimeter-log.csv     # altitude, time
│   ├── accel-log.csv         # G-load, time
│   └── conditions.csv        # temp, pressure, launch metadata
├── biology/
│   ├── germination.csv       # counts, flown vs. control
│   ├── growth-timeseries.csv # measurements over days
│   └── photos/               # dated, labeled images
└── methods/
    └── protocol.md           # detailed enough to repeat the flight
One row, one observation. Keep CSVs tidy: each row is a single measurement, each column a single variable, with a header row of clear names and units.

Step 2 — Describe it

Metadata is what turns files into a findable dataset. At minimum, capture:

FieldExample
Study titleEffect of high-G launch on Arabidopsis germination
OrganismArabidopsis thaliana, Col-0
Factor(s) studiedSpaceflight/launch loads vs. ground control
Assay type(s)Phenotype — germination & growth
Flight parametersPeak G, max altitude, duration, motor class
Sample countsn flown / n control
Date & locationISO date; launch site your site
ContributorsNames / club / institution
Not sure how to phrase a field? Revisit OSDR Training Module 1–3, and mirror an existing OSDR plant study.

Step 3 — Validate

Before you upload, self-check against the readiness list:

  • Every file opens and has clear headers/units
  • Flight log links to biology samples
  • Control group fully documented
  • Methods detailed enough to repeat
  • No sensitive or personal data in files

Step 4 — Submit to OSDR

Head to NASA's repository and follow its submission path:

  • Start at the OSDR portal ↗
  • Review its current submission guidance & contact the data-curation team
  • Provide your metadata + data package
  • Respond to curator feedback; receive your dataset identifier
Heads-up: OSDR's exact submission process and acceptance criteria are set by NASA and can change. Always confirm the current requirements on the official portal before submitting. verify & link current process
