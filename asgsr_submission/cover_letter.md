Dear Program Committee,

Please consider the enclosed abstract, "Botanical sounding rockets as an
accessible space-biology platform: stress-gene expression, seedling growth, and
gravitropism from a high-school and AstroBotany laboratory partnership," for
presentation at the ASGSR Annual Meeting. We are submitting under the
**High School / Middle School** category.

Our work describes a complete, student-led space-biology pipeline across three
sounding-rocket launches: a stress-gene qPCR experiment in flown *Arabidopsis*,
a *Populus tremula* × ectomycorrhiza survival timelapse quantified by automated
image analysis (which returned an honest negative result — the mycorrhizal
seedlings died back), and a gravitropism assay under spectral light. We interpret
our classroom-scale findings against the published orbital "highlight"/ROS stress
response, and we have packaged all data to the ISA model used by NASA's Open
Science Data Repository.

We believe this contribution speaks to ASGSR's mission on two fronts: it presents
original (if preliminary) plant space-biology data, and it demonstrates an
accessible, reproducible model for engaging students in gravitational and space
research. The presenting author is [Author One], a [grade/role] at MadWest High
School.

We confirm that this work is original, not under consideration elsewhere, and
contains no proprietary information that would preclude publication in the
conference proceedings.
